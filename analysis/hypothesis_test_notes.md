# Hypothesis Notes

## Business Problem Statement

The company wants to determine whether the new marketing campaign performs better than the existing campaign in generating customer conversions and revenue. The decision will impact marketing managers, business stakeholders, and future advertising budget allocation.

The primary objective is to improve the conversion rate and overall revenue generated from marketing activities while maintaining a positive return on investment (ROI).

Before recommending a full-scale rollout of the new campaign, potential risks such as lower customer engagement, reduced conversions, increased acquisition costs, and revenue decline must be carefully monitored.

Evidence required includes statistical comparison of campaign performance metrics such as conversion rate, revenue generated, customer response rate, and ROI between the control group and the experimental group.

## Hypothesis Formulation

### Null Hypothesis (H₀)

There is no significant difference between the existing campaign and the new campaign in terms of business performance metrics such as conversion rate, revenue, and customer engagement.

Mathematically:

H₀: μ₁ = μ₂

Where:

* μ₁ = Mean performance of existing campaign
* μ₂ = Mean performance of new campaign


### Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

There is a significant difference between the existing campaign and the new campaign in terms of business performance metrics.

Mathematically:

H₁: μ₁ ≠ μ₂


## Success Metrics

The following metrics will be evaluated:

1. Conversion Rate
2. Revenue Generated
3. Customer Response Rate
4. Return on Investment (ROI)
5. Customer Acquisition Efficiency


## Risks to Monitor

* Reduced conversion rate
* Increased campaign cost
* Lower customer engagement
* Negative impact on revenue
* Poor return on marketing investment



## Recommendation Criteria

The new campaign will be recommended only if:

* Statistical testing indicates a significant improvement.
* Conversion rate is higher than the current campaign.
* Revenue generated increases.
* ROI remains positive.
* No major business risks are observed.


## Assumptions

* Data collected is accurate and complete.
* Control and experimental groups are independent.
* Sample size is sufficient for statistical analysis.
* External market conditions affect both groups equally.


## Expected Outcome

If the experimental campaign demonstrates statistically significant improvement in key performance metrics, it should be considered for broader deployment across future marketing initiatives.
